Offer Logic

At Fresh Tilled Soil, we worked together with OfferLogic (formerly AdAgility), beginning with a product design sprint, and eventually working on parts of their product, a customer engagement platform for marketers to easily create first and third party offers.

  • Design Sprint
  • Visual Design
  • Design System

Defining the Problem

We embarked on a full product design sprint to improve business for OfferLogic and uncovered two key user problems:

Host Partner

“We need another way to increase margins but don’t have the time and resources to devote to it.”

Advertiser

“Our current advertising solutions aren’t working and we don’t know what else to try.”

Mapping Out All Touchpoints

During the design sprint, I created an experience map (off of which I created the first version of the FTS experience map template), charting out the different types of users, their tasks, goals, needs, wants, obstacles, and opportunities for improvement. After the active design sprint sessions, I further developed this with more detail to help clear the path for future design decisions.

Testing Our Assumptions

The end product of this design sprint was a first for our team—a sales demo! We designed it to echo the typical questions/concerns that normally arise during a sales call, depending on the type of prospect. We went ahead and tested it the same way we would any other prototype, running through mock sales pitches with current OfferLogic customers. We received plenty of valuable feedback.

Designing a System

A few months later, we worked with OfferLogic again to tackle a piece of their tool from the internal side. I supported on UX and owned the visual design side for this project, and created a UI kit based on their brand at the time.

Beaucoup Form Fields

We mainly worked on the offer report section, which consists of creating new offer reports based on existing templates, and accessing easily digestible data in one neat report.
